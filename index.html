About

The goal of ThinkTools is to promote interconnectivity amongst students which is transmitted through unscripted social interactions and structured exposure to STEM technology. The workshops are designed to offer introductory computer programming skills that will be applied to industry standard instrumentation, data acquisition, and instrument control problems involving mathematics and science. Students will receive hands-on instruction, STEM experience and participate in project-oriented exploration throughout the workshop. The workshops will utilize technology to connect students to valuable information and provide students useful skills that will better equip them for lifelong learning.




Workshops

While growing up individuals experience a variety of learning environments in which educational processes take place. The environment of the workshop is laid back. We setup it up to be more social than a formal classroom setting. It has an after school program feel to it. The student are able to work with other students in hope of completing their arduino project. The fact that facilitators and participants are both student provides a cohesive nature. The students look up to facilitators because they understand the is the path they need to take in order to achieve educational success. Our goal is to make STEM technology fun for all ages. So, our informal way of teaching these technological concept is very well received by students. The practical applications of the projects and informal inviron are conducive for lifelong learning because student are able to begin learning outside of the classroom and for some student this is the first exposure to learning in an informal environment
